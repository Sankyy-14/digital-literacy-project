# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

A responsible digital presence checklist for first-year students.

---

## DO's ✅

1. **Keep your profiles professional** – Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a bio that reflects your academic/professional interests.
2. **Think before you post** – Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?" If not, don't post it.
3. **Use privacy settings** – Regularly review who can see your posts, stories, and personal information on every platform.
4. **Engage positively and constructively** – Comment and share content that adds value, supports others, or contributes meaningfully to a discussion.
5. **Credit others' work** – Always tag or mention the original creator when sharing someone else's content, photos, or ideas.
6. **Report harmful content** – Use platform reporting tools to flag cyberbullying, misinformation, or harassment you come across.
7. **Keep your email and phone number private** – Avoid posting contact details publicly where anyone can see them.

---

## DON'Ts ❌

1. **Don't share your location in real time** – Posting "I'm at X place right now" publicly can compromise your physical safety.
2. **Don't engage in or ignore cyberbullying** – Neither participating in bullying nor staying silent when you see it is acceptable.
3. **Don't spread unverified information** – Always check a news source before sharing. Misinformation spreads fast and causes real harm.
4. **Don't post anything that could embarrass you professionally** – Offensive jokes, rants, or controversial statements can affect internship and placement prospects years later.
5. **Don't accept friend/follow requests from strangers indiscriminately** – Verify who you are connecting with, especially on platforms like LinkedIn.
6. **Don't use social media during exams or important deadlines** – Compulsive checking hurts focus and academic performance.
7. **Don't share your passwords or OTPs** – No legitimate platform or person will ever ask you for your password in a message.
