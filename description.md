# Task 1 – Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

## Tool Used
**Canva** (canva.com) – Free online design platform

## What the Design Shows

The infographic is titled **"Be Digitally Smart"** and is designed as a one-page visual resource for first-year B.Tech students. It is divided into three main sections:

1. **What is Digital Literacy?** – A brief definition explaining that digital literacy means the ability to find, evaluate, create, and communicate information using digital technologies responsibly and effectively.

2. **Safe Internet Practices** – Icons and short tips on creating strong passwords, avoiding suspicious links, using two-factor authentication, and being careful about sharing personal information online.

3. **Professional Online Presence** – A checklist-style section advising students to keep profiles updated, use a professional photo, and be mindful of what they post publicly — since employers and institutions look at online profiles.

## Reflection

I used Canva because it has ready-made infographic templates that made it easy to get started without any design experience. The drag-and-drop interface was intuitive and I was able to pick a colour scheme and icons that matched the theme. The most challenging part was deciding what to include — there was so much information on digital literacy that fitting it into one page without making it look cluttered required several rounds of editing. The most interesting discovery was how many students underestimate the importance of a professional online presence as early as first year.
