# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India | CSE0001 Digital Literacy Project

---

## General Online Safety

1. **Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, or CVV with anyone:** No bank, payment app, or government authority will ever call or message you asking for these. If someone does, it is a scam.

2. **Verify who you are talking to before any transaction:** Whether buying, selling, or receiving money online, confirm the other person's identity through a video call or a trusted mutual contact.

3. **Do not click on unverified links received via WhatsApp, SMS, or email:** Fraudsters often send fake payment links or KYC update links that steal your credentials. Always open apps directly from the Play Store/App Store.

4. **Check app permissions regularly:** Remove permissions that apps do not genuinely need (e.g., a calculator app asking for access to your contacts or SMS is a red flag).

5. **Use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication (2FA):** Do not reuse passwords across platforms. Use a password manager if needed.

6. **Be careful on public Wi-Fi:** Avoid logging into banking or UPI apps on public Wi-Fi networks. Use mobile data or a trusted VPN.

---

## UPI & Financial Safety (Specific Tips)

7. **Understand the difference between "Send Money" and "Receive Money" on UPI:** Scanning a QR code or clicking "Pay" sends money FROM your account. You never need to enter your PIN to RECEIVE money.

8. **Never scan a QR code to "receive" a payment:** This is the most common UPI scam. Legitimate payments to you require no action on your part, the money simply arrives in your account.

---

## Reporting Channels

If you or someone you know is a victim of cybercrime or online fraud:

- **National Cyber Crime Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- **Cyber Crime Helpline:** **1930** (24x7, available across India)
- **Immediately contact your bank** to freeze the transaction if it happened recently
