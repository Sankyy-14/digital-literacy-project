# Case Study – UPI/Online Payment Fraud

**Cybercrime Type:** UPI/Online Payment Fraud  
**Prepared by:** Sanket Suri | 25BAI10680 | CSE0001 Digital Literacy 

---

## What Is This Crime?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of financial cybercrime where attackers trick victims into sending money or revealing sensitive credentials through deceptive messages, calls, or fake app interfaces. India processes billions of UPI transactions every month, making it one of the most targeted payment systems by fraudsters.

---

## How It Typically Happens – Step by Step

**Scenario:** A college student, Rahul, lists his old laptop for sale on OLX.

1. A stranger contacts Rahul claiming to be interested in buying the laptop.
2. The buyer says he will pay via UPI and asks Rahul to "accept" the money by scanning a QR code.
3. The QR code, instead of sending money *to* Rahul, actually requests money *from* him.
4. Rahul, unfamiliar with how UPI payment requests work, scans the code and enters his UPI PIN thinking he is receiving payment.
5. Rs. 15,000 is instantly debited from Rahul's account.
6. The fraudster disappears and blocks Rahul on all platforms.

**Variation:** Some fraudsters pose as customer care agents, Army personnel, or scholarship officials to create urgency and extract OTPs or PINs over a phone call.

---

## Who Is Usually Targeted?

- First-time online sellers (students, young adults)
- People unfamiliar with how UPI payment requests differ from payment receipts
- College students expecting financial transactions (scholarships, part-time payments)
- Elderly users unfamiliar with digital payment systems

---

## Consequences

- Immediate and often irreversible financial loss
- Psychological distress and loss of trust in digital payment systems
- In some cases, victims are manipulated into making multiple transactions
- Legal and recovery processes are lengthy, with no guarantee of refund
